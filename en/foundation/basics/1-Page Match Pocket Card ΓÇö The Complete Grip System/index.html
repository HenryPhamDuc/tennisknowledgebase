    The Complete Grip System¶

    How to use this card - Print on card stock (heavier paper survives sweat and bag wear) - Laminate for water resistance - Fold to fit your tennis bag pocket (the side pocket, not the racket compartment) - Read between games (during changeovers, not during points) - Glance at the V check before every serve

 THE 2 RULES
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 1. 70/30 RULE: 70% Continental, 30% Semi-Western
 The Continental is the DEFAULT for volleys, slices,
 serves, lobs, overheads, drop shots, approaches, returns.
 The Semi-Western is the FOREHAND groundstroke and return.
 2. 5-SECOND RESET: Use 5 of the 15-25 seconds between points for the
 grip check. Walk → Look → Check → Walk.

 THE 3 GRIPS (V POSITIONS)
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 11 o'clock CONTINENTAL (bevel 2) → 9 shots | 70% DEFAULT
 1 o'clock SEMI-WESTERN (bevel 4) → 2 shots | 30% DEFAULT (forehand)
 2 o'clock WESTERN (bevel 5+) → extreme | NOT 50+ (for the brave)

 THE 9 SHOTS — CONTINENTAL (V at 11)
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 # Shot Description
 ─── ────────────────── ────────────────────────────────────
 1 Forehand volley Block & punch at the net
 2 Backhand volley Block & punch at the net
 3 Forehand slice Carve with open face
 4 Backhand slice Carve with open face
 5 Serve Flat, slice, or kick
 6 Overhead Sane smash
 7 Defensive lob High arc, deep
 8 Drop shot Soft, dies at net
 9 Slice approach Deep slice, follow to net
 +1 Return of serve Block & redirect

 THE 2 SHOTS — SEMI-WESTERN (V at 1)
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 # Shot Description
 ─── ─────────────────────── ────────────────────────────────
 1 Forehand groundstroke Forearm rotation, low → high
 2 Forehand return Same grip, 1 o'clock V

 THE 4 CONTACT POINTS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 HEEL PAD = back bevel (5), the ANCHOR
 KNUCKLE = bevel 2, the POINTER
 THUMB = along bevel 5, the STABILIZER (NOT wrapped)
 INDEX = along bevel 1, the TRIGGER

 THE 3-PHASE PRESSURE
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 PHASE 1 (Setup): 2-3/10 "Baby bird" Soft hand, light racket
 PHASE 2 (Swing): 3-4/10 "Slingshot stone" Loose swing, free arm
 PHASE 3 (Contact): 7-9/10 "Wrist lock" Firm grip, stable face
 EXCEPTION: Drop shot stays at 2-3/10 (soft at contact)

 THE 4 POINT TYPES + GRIP CHANGES
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 Serving point = 0 changes (Continental stays for serve + volley + slice)
 Returning point = 1-3 changes (forehand return = Semi-Western)
 Net point = 0-1 changes (Continental stays at net)
 Mixed point = 2-4 changes (the most common)

 THE 8 GRIP TRANSITIONS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 Pre-serve → Continental 1 sec
 Serve → Forehand → Continental → Semi-W 1 sec (bevel walk 2)
 Forehand → Backhand → Semi-W → Continental 1 sec (bevel walk 2)
 Backhand → Forehand → Continental → Semi-W 1 sec (bevel walk 2)
 Approach → Volley → Semi-W → Continental 1 sec (bevel walk 2)
 Forehand → F return → Semi-W (no change) 0 sec
 C → F return (def) → Continental → Eastern 0.5 sec (bevel walk 1)
 Back to home base → Any → Continental 0.5-1 sec

 THE 5-SECOND RESET (between points)
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 0-2 sec WALK to the line
 2-3 sec LOOK at the V (11, 12, 1, or 2 o'clock?)
 3-4 sec CHECK the bevel (2, 3, 4, or 5?)
 4-5 sec WALK to the position (bevel walk if needed)

 THE 4 PICKUP METHODS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 HAMMER PICKUP Hold out → lay hand → slide down → close (Continental)
 For Continental, the universal pickup
 SHAKE HANDS Pretend to shake hands with the racket
 For Eastern/Semi-Western, natural feel
 BEVEL WALK Start at Continental (11), walk 1-2 bevels clockwise
 For changing during a match, 1 second
 INDEX FINGER Extend the index finger down the side
 For quick check during a match

 THE 3 SOUND CUES
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 "Bộp" = solid contact (good)
 "Bịch" = lazy block (too soft)
 "Phập" = clean slice (good slice)

 THE 4 PROTECTED JOINTS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 ✓ WRIST (no wrist snap, natural underspin from open face)
 ✓ ELBOW (pronation, not wrist snap, no varus torque)
 ✓ SHOULDER (6-inch punch, not full swing)
 ✓ KNEE (via 70% sprint on slice approach)

 THE 4 NUMBERS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 Time per change: 0.5-1.0 sec (50+) vs 0.2-0.5 sec (30)
 Changes per point: 1-2 (50+) vs 1-2 (30)
 Fumbling per match: 6-7.5 min (50+) vs 1-2 min (30)
 Win rate impact: 20% (50+) vs 5% (30)

 THE 6 COMMON ERRORS
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 1. Fumbling 3+ sec (fix: 5-second reset)
 2. Western on 50+ body (fix: 1 o'clock is the 50+ default)
 3. Continental on forehand (fix: V at 1 for forehand)
 4. Forget to change at net (fix: bevel walk before the net)
 5. Wrist snap for topspin (fix: forearm rotation, not wrist)
 6. No 5-second reset (fix: walk-look-check-walk)

 THE 5 CUES TO REMEMBER
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 1. "Continental is home base" — Return to Continental between points
 2. "5-second reset" — Walk → Look → Check → Walk
 3. "V at 11 = Continental" — The V is the steering wheel
 4. "Bevel walk in 1 second" — 1-2 bevels from Continental
 5. "Forearm rotation, not wrist" — Joint-friendly topspin

 THE 50+ LONGEVITY RULE
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 • The grip that RESPECTS the joints plays for 20+ years
 • The grip that LOADS the joints plays for 10 years
 • Continental = respects wrist, elbow, shoulder (4 protected)
 • Western = loads wrist, elbow, shoulder (4 at risk)
 • Semi-Western = respects joints (forearm rotation, not wrist)

 THE MASTER CUE
 ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 "Continental is home base. Semi-Western is forehand. 5-second reset.
 Baby bird → Slingshot stone → Wrist lock. At 50+, the player
 with this card plays 20 years. The player without fumbles
 7 minutes per match."

    HOW TO USE THIS CARD¶

    Before the Match¶

    Step What to do
    1 Read the card on the way to the court
    2 Pick the 3-5 cues that fit today's situation
    3 Carry the card in the side pocket of your bag

    During the Match¶

    Moment What to do
    Before each serve Glance at the V (11 or 1 o'clock?)
    Between points Use the 5-second reset (walk-look-check-walk)
    At changeover Read 1 section of the card (15 sec)
    After a bad point Find the cue that fits (e.g., "V at 11 = Continental")
    After a good point Find the cue that fits (e.g., "Forearm rotation, not wrist")

    After the Match¶

    Step What to do
    1 Review: which cues worked? Which didn't?
    2 Note: which errors did you make? (6 common errors)
    3 Pick 1 cue to focus on next match
    4 Carry the card again (or print a fresh one)

    Print Specifications¶

    Spec Recommendation
    Paper Card stock (110-150 lb), matte (less glare)
    Size 4.25" × 5.5" (fits side pocket) or 5" × 7" (more readable)
    Color Black text on white (most readable); red for "5 cues to remember" (eye-catching)
    Laminate Yes (matte laminate, 3-5 mil thickness)
    Fold Bi-fold (8.5" × 11" → 5.5" × 8.5") or tri-fold (4.25" × 11" → 4.25" × 3.7")
    Pocket Side pocket of tennis bag (NOT the racket compartment)
